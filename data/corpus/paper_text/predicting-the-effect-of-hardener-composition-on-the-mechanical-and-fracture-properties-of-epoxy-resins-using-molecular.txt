[CONCEPTS]
- Coarse-grained molecular dynamics (CG-MD): a simulation approach that maps groups of atoms to coarse-grained (CG) beads to accelerate molecular dynamics while retaining chemistry-specific interactions. Bonded interactions are parametrized by Iterative Boltzmann Inversion (IBI); nonbonded interactions are renormalized to reproduce target atomistic quantities (density, elastic modulus, yield strength, Debye–Waller factor).
- Epoxy network chemistry: networks formed by curing a difunctional epoxide resin, bisphenol A diglycidyl ether (DGEBA), with diamine hardeners. Hardener species considered: diamine 4,4′-methylenebis(cyclohexylamine) (PACM, relatively rigid) and polyether diamines commercially called Jeffamine (flexible, with tunable chain length).
- Cross-link density (ρ_{CL}): number of covalent cross-links per unit volume in the cured network; controls network strand length, stiffness, and ductility.
- Mechanical observables: elastic modulus (E; slope of stress–strain in small strain), yield strength (σ_y; characteristic stress at yield), plateau stress (σ_p; average stress in post-yield plateau), ultimate strength (σ_max; maximum tensile stress), strain at ultimate strength (ε_{σ_max}), stretch ratio (λ; dimensionless measure of chain drawing in the post-yield regime), fracture energy (G; energy per unit area to propagate a crack), and fracture toughness (K_Ic; stress intensity factor at fracture).
- Void metrics: mean void diameter (d or MVD) defined as the average largest-sphere-diameter that can traverse void space; normalized void volume = total void volume divided by sample volume. Voronoi-based geometry analysis (Zeo++) is used for void identification and sizing.
- Bond formation / scission rules in CG-MD: reactive bond formation is implemented by geometric proximity thresholds between designated epoxide (E) and amine (N) beads; bond scission is implemented by removing a bond when its instantaneous length exceeds a calibrated multiple of its equilibrium bond length.

[MECHANISMS]
- Increasing PACM mole fraction increases network stiffness and yield strength because PACM is a relatively rigid diamine that produces shorter effective chain segments and a higher effective cross-link rigidity, thereby increasing elastic modulus E and σ_y.
- Increasing Jeffamine chain length increases ductility and fracture energy because longer flexible segments allow more chain drawing, fibril formation, and larger void growth during post-yield deformation, thus dissipating more energy before catastrophic failure.
- Short Jeffamine chains (small molecular weight Jeffamines) increase yield strength relative to long Jeffamines because shorter segments yield a denser distribution of cross-link points per unit volume (higher effective ρ_{CL}) and less extensibility, concentrating stress and increasing σ_y.
- During uniaxial tensile loading, bond scission predominantly occurs at amine–epoxide connections (amine-side scission) and is concentrated in the strain-hardening / post-yield region; a small fraction of total amine connections (∼2.9% at maximum stress in representative systems) fail before macroscopic separation.
- Larger mean void diameters (d) correlate with more ductile failure modes: long Jeffamine chains lead to continuous MVD growth during stretch, fibril formation along the loading direction, and delayed macroscopic splitting; short Jeffamine networks show abrupt MVD growth near final failure and brittle splitting.
- Cross-link network heterogeneity (bimodal distribution of strand lengths for long Jeffamines) promotes larger plastic zones and higher fracture energy; intermediate-length Jeffamines (D800 and D2000) combined with PACM balance stiffness and energy dissipation to overcome strength–toughness tradeoffs.
- Fracture energy at the continuum scale can be estimated from molecular observables (d, σ_max, σ_p, λ) and elastic constants (C_{11}, C_{66}) via a crazing-based continuum model (Brown-type), connecting nanoscale void/fibril formation to macroscopic G.

[METHOD]
1. Chemistry-specific coarse-grained model construction:
   - Map atomistic structures to CG beads: DGEBA mapped to 5 beads (3 bead types due to symmetry), PACM to 4 beads (2 bead types), each Jeffamine repeat unit represented by 3 bead types; Jeffamine chain lengths studied correspond to nominal molecular weights/repeat counts labeled D230 (x=3), D400 (x=6), D800 (x=13), D2000 (x=33), D4000 (x=68).
   - Parametrize bonded interactions (bonds, angles, dihedrals) using Iterative Boltzmann Inversion (IBI) to reproduce atomistic bond/angle/dihedral distributions.
   - Parametrize nonbonded interactions by energy renormalization coupled with a Gaussian process metamodel to match atomistic targets: density (ρ), elastic modulus (E), yield strength (σ_y), and Debye–Waller factor (u^2).
   - Validate CG model against all-atom (AA) and ReaxFF benchmarks for stress–strain response and static properties.

2. System assembly and initial equilibration:
   - Pack CG beads into a simulation cell to achieve ~30,000 beads per system (all compositions normalized to roughly equal bead count).
   - Initial high-temperature, high-pressure NPT equilibration: temperature held at T=1000 K while pressure reduced from 100 atm to 1 atm in three staged steps; at each stage equilibrate for 0.4 ns.

3. Cross-linking procedure (Polymatic-based algorithm):
   - After each equilibration step compute pairwise root-mean-square (RMS) distance between epoxide (E) and amine (N) reactive CG beads. If RMS < r_form = 15 Å, form a new bonded interaction set (bond, angle, dihedral) between that E and N bead; relabel reacted E bead to nonreactive.
   - Allow primary amine beads to form up to two connections (label N, then N2).
   - After each successful cross-link perform energy minimization. After every 20 newly formed cross-links equilibrate the structure under NPT at T=1000 K and P=100 atm for 20 ps to relax.
   - Stop cross-linking when no eligible E–N pairs remain or when no new bonds form for 20 consecutive equilibration cycles. The procedure yields near-complete cross-link conversion (>98%) while preventing first- and second-order loops by disallowing double binding of same epoxide to the same amine during early stages.

4. Post-cross-link annealing and equilibration:
   - Apply small annealing cycle to relax residual stresses: in four consecutive NPT phases (each 2 ns) reduce T and P from (800 K, 100 atm) down to (300 K, 0 atm) to obtain ambient-condition structures.

5. Mechanical testing via CG-MD:
   - Perform uniaxial tensile deformation at T = 300 K with strain rate \dot{\epsilon} = 5×10^8 s^{-1} by extending the simulation cell in the axial direction to engineering strains up to 500% while keeping perpendicular cross-sectional dimensions fixed (approximate plane-stress-like conditions).
   - During tensile loading, every 5 timesteps (Δt = 4 fs; i.e., every 20 fs) examine predesignated reactive bond lengths. If bond length l > l_{cut} = 1.525 × l_{eq} (where l_{eq} is bond equilibrium length), delete the bond (and associated angles/dihedrals) to model bond scission; the cutoff l_{cut} is calibrated by matching stress–strain response to ReaxFF results.
   - Record stress–strain data; compute elastic modulus E as slope dσ/dε for ε ≤ 0.02, yield strength σ_y from averaged stress near yield (~10% strain), plateau stress σ_p averaged over 20%–80% strain region, ultimate strength σ_max and the corresponding strain ε_{σ_max}, and the stretch ratio λ determined from the strain at which stress equals average of σ_p and σ_max.
   - Compute elastic constants C_{11} and C_{66} by applying small uniaxial/shear strains up to ε=0.1 in increments of 0.02 and taking linear slopes.

6. Structural and failure analysis:
   - Quantify voids by computing Voronoi decomposition using Zeo++ to obtain mean void diameter (d), void-size distributions, and normalized void volume as functions of applied strain.
   - Track bond scission statistics by species (PACM–DGEBA vs Jeffamine–DGEBA) and cumulative percentage of amine bonds broken as a function of strain.
   - Compute cross-link density ρ_{CL} = N_{CL}/V from number of formed cross-links N_{CL} and sample volume V after equilibration. Analyze distribution of distances between consecutive cross-links ⟨d_{CL}⟩ to assess network heterogeneity.
   - Convert molecular fracture energy G to fracture toughness K_Ic using plane-stress relation K_Ic = √(E G) for comparison with experimental K_Ic data when applicable.

[KEY EQUATIONS / MODELS]
- Bond scission cutoff:
  - Equation:
    \[ l_{\text{cut}} = 1.525 \, l_{\text{eq}} \]
  - Symbols: \(l_{\text{cut}}\) = bond-length cutoff for scission; \(l_{\text{eq}}\) = equilibrium bond length of that CG bond.
  - Use: remove a bonded interaction (bond, angle, dihedral) during tensile simulation if instantaneous bond length exceeds \(l_{\text{cut}}\); calibrated against ReaxFF atomistic bond-breaking behavior.

- Reactive bond formation criterion (cross-linking):
  - Equation:
    \[ r_{\text{form}} = 15\ \text{\AA} \]
  - Symbols: \(r_{\text{form}}\) = RMS distance threshold between epoxide (E) and amine (N) beads for forming a new cross-link.
  - Use: If RMS(E,N) < \(r_{\text{form}}\) during cross-linking cycles, create a bonded interaction set to form covalent cross-links.

- Cross-link density:
  - Equation:
    \[ \rho_{CL} = \frac{N_{CL}}{V} \]
  - Symbols: \(\rho_{CL}\) = cross-link density (number per unit volume); \(N_{CL}\) = total number of cross-links in the system; \(V\) = system volume after equilibration.
  - Use: quantify how network architecture changes with hardener composition and Jeffamine length; used to correlate to E, σ_y, and void metrics.

- Elastic modulus extraction:
  - Equation:
    \[ E = \frac{d\sigma}{d\epsilon}\Bigg|_{\epsilon \le 0.02} \]
  - Symbols: \(E\) = elastic modulus (Pa); \(\sigma\) = engineering stress; \(\epsilon\) = engineering strain.
  - Use: determine small-strain stiffness by linear fit to stress–strain up to 2% strain.

- Elastic constants used in fracture model:
  - Equations (computational procedure):
    \[ C_{11} = \frac{d\sigma_x}{d\epsilon_x}\Bigg|_{0 \le \epsilon_x \le 0.1}, \qquad C_{66} = \frac{d\tau_{xy}}{d\gamma_{xy}}\Bigg|_{0 \le \gamma_{xy} \le 0.1} \]
  - Symbols: \(C_{11}\) = axial elastic constant; \(C_{66}\) = in-plane shear elastic constant; \(\sigma_x\) = axial stress; \(\epsilon_x\) = axial strain; \(\tau_{xy}\) = shear stress; \(\gamma_{xy}\) = shear strain.
  - Use: small-strain linear-response constants inserted into continuum fracture energy estimate.

- Continuum fracture-energy estimate (Brown-type crazing model used to map nanoscale fibril/void metrics to macroscopic fracture energy):
  - Equation (form used to estimate fracture energy from molecular observables):
    \[ G \;=\; 2\, d \,\lambda \left( \frac{\sigma_{\max}^2}{C_{11}} \;+\; \frac{\sigma_{p}^2}{C_{66}} \right) \]
  - Symbols: \(G\) = fracture energy (J m^{-2}); \(d\) = strain-averaged mean void diameter (m); \(\lambda\) = stretch ratio (dimensionless); \(\sigma_{\max}\) = ultimate tensile strength (Pa); \(\sigma_p\) = plateau stress (Pa); \(C_{11}\), \(C_{66}\) = elastic constants (Pa).
  - Use: compute macroscopic fracture energy by combining molecular-scale void/fibril parameters with continuum elastic response; units check: Pa^2 / Pa × m = Pa·m = J·m^{-2}.

- Conversion to fracture toughness (plane stress approximation):
  - Equation:
    \[ K_{Ic} = \sqrt{E\,G} \]
  - Symbols: \(K_{Ic}\) = fracture toughness (Pa·m^{1/2}); \(E\) = elastic modulus (Pa); \(G\) = fracture energy (J m^{-2}).
  - Use: convert computed G to a stress-intensity-like metric for comparison with experimental fracture toughness data obtained under plane-stress-like conditions.

[RESULTS]
- Mechanical property trends with hardener composition:
  - Increasing PACM mole fraction from 0% to 80% at fixed Jeffamine length increases elastic modulus \(E\) and yield strength \(σ_y\) monotonically; PACM is the dominant contributor to stiffness.
  - At 0% PACM (100% Jeffamine), elastic modulus decreases systematically with increasing Jeffamine length: D230-00 has ~95% higher \(E\) than D4000-00 (same bead count normalization). At 80% PACM the difference between D230-80 and D4000-80 reduced to ~50%.
  - Ultimate strength \(σ_{\max}\) remained roughly invariant with PACM fraction for all Jeffamine lengths; however, strain at ultimate \(ε_{σ_{\max}}\) increases strongly with Jeffamine chain length (longer chains → larger failure strain and higher fracture energy).

- Bond scission and void evolution:
  - Amine–epoxide bonds are the primary broken bonds during tensile failure; ~2.9% of total amine bonds fail at maximum stress in representative systems.
  - Short-Jeffamine systems (e.g., D230-40) exhibit sudden bond-break bursts concentrated near \(ε \approx 1\)–2 with a steep MVD spike and brittle sample splitting. Long-Jeffamine systems (e.g., D4000-40) show gradual, stepwise bond breakage during strain-hardening, continuous MVD growth, and fibril formation carrying stress, leading to ductile failure.

- Cross-link density and network heterogeneity:
  - Measured ρ_{CL} correlates positively and approximately linearly with \(E\) and \(σ_y\); higher ρ_{CL} → higher stiffness and yield strength.
  - Distribution of distances between consecutive cross-links ⟨d_{CL}⟩: short Jeffamines (D230–D800) show near-normal distributions; long Jeffamines (D2000, D4000) show bimodal/heavily skewed distributions with ranges from ~10 to ~130 Å, indicating significant heterogeneity that promotes larger plastic zones and energy dissipation.

- Fracture-energy predictions and comparisons:
  - Fracture energy \(G\) increases strongly with Jeffamine chain length at fixed low PACM fraction: D4000-00 has ~9.5× larger \(G\) than D230-00.
  - Simulation-to-experiment comparison: for D400 (shorter Jeffamine) systems with selected PACM fractions, the computed fracture energy converted to \(K_{Ic}\) via \(K_{Ic}=\sqrt{E G}\) yields values within ~20% of experimental data reported at low temperature (153 K) to partially offset high simulation strain-rate effects. Example: G_{sim} = 146.08 J m^{-2} vs G_{exp} = 187.01 J m^{-2} for one composition.
  - Optimal composition: Ashby-style trade-off plots of toughness vs stiffness or toughness vs yield strength show intermediate-length Jeffamines (D800, D2000) combined with PACM outperform many other combinations; these systems balance stiffness from PACM and ductility/toughening from Jeffamine chains, mitigating the strength–toughness tradeoff.

- Two explicit comparative statements:
  - D4000-00 vs D230-00: fracture energy of D4000-00 ≈ 9.5× fracture energy of D230-00 at identical bead-normalized composition, driven primarily by enhanced fibril formation and continuous void growth in D4000.
  - Simulation vs experimental benchmark: computed G for a D400-based epoxy composition was within ~20% of reported experimental G at 153 K (G_{sim} = 146.08 J m^{-2}; G_{exp} = 187.01 J m^{-2}), demonstrating quantitative predictive capability despite coarse graining and high strain rates.

[LIMITATIONS & EDGE CASES]
- Coarse-graining approximations and loss of chemical detail: CG beads lump several atoms, and parametrization choices (IBI for bonded terms and renormalized nonbonded potentials) may not capture subtle chemical effects (e.g., exact bond dissociation energetics, reaction-induced local chemistry).
- Bond scission modeled by geometric cutoff rather than explicit bond-breaking potential energy surface: bonds are deleted when length exceeds \(l_{\text{cut}} = 1.525\, l_{\text{eq}}\); this rule is calibrated to ReaxFF but does not model energy barriers or temperature-activated kinetics of bond rupture explicitly.
- High strain-rate bias: tensile simulations used \(\dot{\epsilon} = 5\times10^{8}\ \text{s}^{-1}\), orders of magnitude above experimental quasi-static rates, which tends to increase apparent strengths and failure strains; experimental comparisons were performed at low temperature to partially counterbalance rate effects but residual discrepancies remain.
- Finite-size and defect absence: simulation cells contain ~30,000 beads and are effectively defect-free; macroscopic specimens and real systems contain defects/inhomogeneities and polydispersity that can reduce toughness or change failure modes.
- Cross-link completeness and topology constraints: the Polymatic protocol targets near-full conversion (>98%) and disallows certain local loop formations during early cross-linking steps; real curing may leave unreacted groups or a different loop population.
- Limited composition space: studied PACM mole fractions were 0%, 20%, 40%, 60%, 80% (in 20% increments) and Jeffamine lengths up to D4000 (x=68); extrapolation beyond these ranges (e.g., >80% PACM, Jeffamine lengths ≫ D4000, or gradients of hardener concentration) should be treated cautiously.
- Environmental and chemical effects omitted: moisture uptake, oxidation, plasticizer effects, and additives (e.g., rubber particles, thermoplastics, nanoparticles) were not included and can fundamentally alter fracture mechanisms.
- Applicability of fracture model: the Brown-type continuum fracture model assumes crazing-like fibril formation and plane-stress-like conditions; in cases where microvoiding or shear-dominated failure dominates, the model may misestimate G.

[VARIABLES & RANGES]
- \( \text{PACM fraction} \): 0%–80% mole fraction of total curing agent, varied in 20% increments; controlled by initial stoichiometric mixing of hardener beads.
- Jeffamine identifiers and nominal chain lengths:
  - \( \text{D230} \) : \(x=3\) repeat units (nominal molecular weight ≈ 230 g mol^{-1}),
  - \( \text{D400} \) : \(x=6\) (≈ 400 g mol^{-1}),
  - \( \text{D800} \) : \(x=13\) (≈ 800 g mol^{-1}),
  - \( \text{D2000} \) : \(x=33\) (≈ 2000 g mol^{-1}),
  - \( \text{D4000} \) : \(x=68\) (≈ 4000 g mol^{-1}).
  - Jeffamine length controlled by number of repeat CG subunits per chain in the initial packed structure.
- \( V \) (system volume): ~set by equilibrated simulation cell for ≈30,000 beads; used to compute \( \rho_{CL} = N_{CL} / V \).
- \( N_{CL} \) (number of cross-links): resulting from Polymatic cross-linking algorithm; typical conversion >98% of reactive groups.
- \( \rho_{CL} \) (cross-link density): varies with Jeffamine length and PACM fraction; higher for short Jeffamines and high PACM mole fraction; units: number nm^{-3} (computed from N_{CL}/V).
- \( r_{\text{form}} \): bond formation threshold during cross-linking, fixed at 15 Å (1.5 nm); used as geometric criterion for creating E–N bonds.
- \( l_{\text{eq}} \) and \( l_{\text{cut}} \): equilibrium bond length depends on CG bond type; cut for scission \(l_{\text{cut}} = 1.525 \, l_{\text{eq}}\).
- \( \dot{\epsilon} \) (strain rate): 5 × 10^{8} s^{-1} for uniaxial tensile tests; controlled by imposing time-dependent extension of axial simulation cell.
- Temperature and pressure schedules:
  - Initial cross-linking equilibration: T = 1000 K, pressure progressively reduced from 100 atm to 1 atm in three steps, each equilibrated 0.4 ns.
  - Post-cross-link relaxation: occasional NPT at T = 1000 K, P = 100 atm for 20 ps after every 20 cross-links.
  - Annealing: four NPT phases, each 2 ns, reducing from (T,P) = (800 K, 100 atm) to (300 K, 0 atm).
  - Mechanical testing: uniaxial deformation at T = 300 K, P = 0 atm (cross-section held fixed).
- Time-step and bond-check interval: MD time-step Δt = 4 fs; bond-length checks performed every 5 timesteps (every 20 fs).
- Strain measurement and analysis windows:
  - Elastic modulus \(E\) computed from stress–strain slope for ε ≤ 0.02.
  - Yield strength \(σ_y\) from average stress near yield (~10% strain).
  - Plateau stress \(σ_p\) averaged over 20%–80% strain region.
  - Ultimate strength \(σ_{\max}\) and strain at ultimate \(ε_{σ_{\max}}\) read from stress–strain curve peak.
  - Stretch ratio \(λ\) determined from strain at which stress equals average of σ_p and σ_max.
- Void metrics:
  - Mean void diameter \(d\) (MVD) computed by Voronoi analysis over deformed structures (units: Å or nm); strain-averaged MVD obtained over the regime where MVD reaches a near-constant plateau (typical region varies by composition).
  - Normalized void volume = total void volume / V (dimensionless).
- Fracture-energy and fracture-toughness metrics:
  - \( G \) (fracture energy): computed via Brown-type model using \(d\), \(σ_{\max}\), \(σ_p\), \(λ\), \(C_{11}\), \(C_{66}\). Simulated G examples: G_{sim} ≈ 146.08 J m^{-2} (example composition).
  - \( K_{Ic} \): computed as \( \sqrt{E G} \) for plane-stress conversion; used to compare against experimental K_Ic values (experimental G_{exp} example: ≈187.01 J m^{-2} for one benchmark composition).
